Kai Lim

Research Assistant · Wildlife & Marine Biology

I'm a research assistant based in Singapore, currently working alongside Dr. Chen Wei at the Department of Biological Sciences. My work centers on rare and critically endangered species animals that exist at the very edge of survival.

My role involves field data collection, literature review, species population analysis, and supporting ongoing conservation research. While I'm still early in my academic path, the work has already taken me deep into questions I didn't expect: What does it mean for a species to be functionally invisible? What do we lose when an animal disappears before we understood it?

Most of what I study will never make headlines. These are quiet disappearances animals with shrinking ranges, collapsing populations, and no public advocates. I keep these field notes partly as a record, and partly because writing about a species feels like one small act of witness.

The rarest animals are not those no one has seen they are those no one is watching.

Outside of research, I'm interested in how science communicates with the public — and how digital tools might carry conservation stories further than academic journals ever could.
